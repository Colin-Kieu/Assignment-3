Job description: Software devlopers are the ones who create software for businesses or companies to perform a specific task on the computer or electronic devices. There is alot of things that can fall under a Software developer, things as creating a game, creating a website for a company and making applications. Software devlopers can create almost anything and can be tailored for every task. Their responsibilities include: to create and test software to the users or clients needs, update and upgrade software for their existing systems and keep close watch on applications to see if they are performing.

Education: Most of the time, a bachelors degreee in computor science and or software engineering is required.

College And University Programs: This is a program from UOttawa: UOttawa offers multiple programs that include computer science and any type of engineering. However I'll be looking over the computer science and sofware engineering programs. For Computer science, the requirements to get into their program is English or French 4U, Advanced Functions 4U, Calculus and Vectors 4U and an average of Low to Mid 90s. for software engineering, you'll need English or French 4U, Advanced Functions 4U, Calculus and Vectors 4U and any of the sciences 4U (biology, chemistry, physics, computing), Your average will need to be in the high 80s. The required first year courses you'll need to take are Digital Systems I(ITI 1100), Introduction to Computing I(ITI 1120), Introduction to Computing II(ITI 1121), Calculus I (MAT 1320, Calculus for the Life Sciences I (MAT 1330). You'll also need to take Calculus II (MAT 1322), Calculus for the Life Sciences II (MAT 1332), Introduction to Linear Algebra (MAT 1341) and Discrete Mathematics for Computing. Other types of courses required to take in this program are things like, databases, programming languages and courses similar to those. (MAT 1348)

Course Duration: this course usually takes 4-5 depending on where you go and what you take.
